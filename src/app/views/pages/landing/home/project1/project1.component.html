Modelling Susceptibility and Generating Rainfall-Threshold for Rainfall-Induced Landslide

One of the most exposed areas to rainfall-induced landslides is the BLISTT area of the Cordillera Administrative Region, which experienced nine out of ten of the country's greatest 24-hour rainfall totals during tropical cyclones. The MULAT-BLISTT, which stands for Modelling and Understanding Landslide Events in BLISTT (Baguio, La Trinidad, Itogon, Sablan, Tuba, Tublay), is a research program funded by the DOST-PCIEERD. The program's objective is to increase disaster resilience in accordance with R.A. 10121, the "Philippine Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Act of 2010," and the UN 2030 Sustainable Development Goal.
